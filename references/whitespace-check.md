# Whitespace Check

A final-pass audit on any strategy brief to verify the positioning is genuinely unique. Run after the brief passes all other quality gates.

## What It Tests

The whitespace check answers one question: **Could any competitor adopt this positioning without changing their product, their audience, or their operations?**

If yes, the positioning isn't in whitespace. It's in shared territory with a paint job.

## The Process

### Step 1: Extract the Claims

Pull every positioning claim from the brief:
- The proposition (rallying cry)
- The emotional core
- The audience definition
- The key insight
- The "What We're Not Doing" list (each exclusion is an implicit claim)

### Step 2: Run the Competitor Gauntlet

For each top competitor (3-5), test EACH claim:

```
WHITESPACE AUDIT
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Claim: [extracted claim]

Competitor A: [name]
  Could they say this? Y/N
  Could they DO this (product exists or could ship in 6 months)? Y/N
  Would they WANT to (fits their brand/audience)? Y/N
  → If all three Y: NOT WHITESPACE. Shared territory.
  → If any N: explain which one blocks them and why.

Competitor B: [name]
  [same questions]

...
```

### Step 3: Find the Unique Intersection

Most positioning has 3-5 claims. Competitors can usually match 1-2 of them. The whitespace is the COMBINATION that no single competitor can replicate.

Map it:
```
CLAIM INTERSECTION MAP
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                    Comp A   Comp B   Comp C   Comp D
Claim 1             ✓        ✗        ✓        ✗
Claim 2             ✗        ✓        ✗        ✗
Claim 3             ✗        ✗        ✗        ✗
Claim 4             ✓        ✓        ✗        ✓
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Unique combo:       [which claims, together, are unmatched?]
```

### Step 4: Stress the Time Dimension

Whitespace today might be shared territory in 12 months. For each unique claim:
- How long would it take the nearest competitor to copy this? (weeks / months / years / structurally impossible)
- What would they have to change to get there? (brand refresh / product build / audience shift / regulatory change)
- Rate the defensibility: **Structural** (can't copy without becoming a different company), **Temporal** (6-12 month head start), or **Cosmetic** (they could ship this next quarter)

### Step 5: Verdict

```
WHITESPACE VERDICT
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
UNIQUE CLAIMS (no competitor can match):
  [list]

TEMPORAL ADVANTAGES (head start, not permanent):
  [list]

SHARED TERRITORY (competitors already here or close):
  [list]

OVERALL: [IN WHITESPACE / PARTIALLY / NOT IN WHITESPACE]

If NOT: what would need to change in the brief to get there?
```

## Scoring

| Dimension | 10 | 7 | 4 | 1 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Uniqueness | No single competitor can match the full positioning. At least 2 claims are structurally unique. | 1 structurally unique claim, rest are temporal advantages. | All claims are temporal advantages only. | A competitor could run this brief as-is. |
| Defensibility | Structural barriers protect the positioning for 2+ years. | 12-month head start on key claims. | 6-month window before competitors close the gap. | No barriers. |
| Specificity | The whitespace is named, mapped, and traceable to product/audience decisions. | Whitespace identified but vaguely bounded. | "We think this is unique" without competitive proof. | No whitespace analysis performed. |

**Threshold: 7/10 on each dimension.** Below 7 on Uniqueness = rewrite the proposition. Below 7 on Defensibility = flag the timeline risk to the user.
