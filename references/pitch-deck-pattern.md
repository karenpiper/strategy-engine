# C&T Pitch Deck Pattern

Extracted from the user Alexander's pitches: Bumble (candidate assignment), Anthropic RFP (55 slides), Xbox Game Pass (125 slides). This is the canonical structure for C&T pitch decks.

## The Universal Structure

### ACT 1: THE SETUP (Strategy owns)
1. **Cover** — Partnership framing (C&T x Client)
2. **Executive Summary** — The strategic reframe, stated upfront. One page. The whole argument compressed. C&T doesn't make clients wait: the answer is on page 2.
3. **Team** — Senior leadership with photos, bios, roles. Proves the people in the room stay through execution.
4. **Agenda/TOC** — Clean sections (typically: Strategy → Creative → Production)

### ACT 2: THE STRATEGY (the user owns, ~10-15 slides max)
5. **The Evolution / The Shift** — What's changing for the client. Big, bold, declarative.
   - Xbox: "From SKU to Tier, Subscription to Membership, Console owners to Anyone"
   - Anthropic: "From Intellectual Partner to Critical Infrastructure"
6. **The Audience** — Sharp portrait, not demographics. Behavior and belief.
   - Xbox: "A growth audience that isn't hard to find, but is hard to win"
   - Anthropic: Enterprise decision makers + decision influencers (two personas)
7. **The Tension** — Two-column format. The strategic problem lands here.
   - Anthropic: "Most AI helps companies do generic tasks" vs "Helping experts solve their hardest problems"
   - Xbox: 3x "It's hard because / But the truth is" reframes
   - Bumble: Brand DNA vs Cultural Truth (created to empower women / but women's romantic experience is more fraught than ever)
8. **The Strategic Shift** — One bold line. The reframe that changes everything.
   - Xbox: "From More Games to More Gaming"
   - Bumble: "From a brand people recognize to a brand people live by"
9. **The Core Truth / Insight** — The audience truth that unlocks the positioning.
   - Xbox: "They don't just love gaming, they love what they get out of it"
   - Bumble: "Being a romantic is the original human superpower; it unlocks creativity by inviting us to find fantasy in the mundane"
   - Often supported by cultural proof (tweets, quotes, real human signals)
10. **The Positioning / Coined Concept** — The line.
    - Xbox: "Get More From Your Gaming"
    - Anthropic: "Thinking Together"
11. **The False Choice / Resolution** — What the positioning resolves. (Optional but powerful.)
    - Anthropic: Organizational intelligence OR expert problem solving → Claude delivers both
12. **Operationalization** — How the strategy becomes real. Layers, systems, frameworks.
    - Anthropic: 4-layer system (Brand Mission → Operational Platform → Vertical Translation → Commercial Proof)
    - Xbox: Audience-specific "More Gaming" expressions

### ACT 3: THE EXPRESSION (Team owns, the user directs)
13. **Vertical Translation** — How the positioning plays across audiences/verticals
14. **Persona Messaging** — Awareness → Consideration → Evaluation per audience
15. **Creative Executions** — Mockups, campaign concepts, visual expressions
16. **Brand Direction** — Visual identity, mood, tone, look and feel (VD)
17. **Product/UX Concepts** — Key screens, flows, interactions (UX)
18. **Design System Thinking** — Components, tokens, motion principles (Creative Tech + VD)

### ACT 4: THE CLOSE (Delivery owns)
19. **Approach & Timeline** — How we'd execute the engagement
20. **Team & Staffing** — Who does what, allocation
21. **Appendix** — Case studies, credentials, additional detail

## Key Rules

1. **The strategy section is ~10-15 slides max.** Everything else serves it or executes against it.
2. **Every strategy deck uses a two-column tension.** Reality vs. Truth. From → To. DNA vs. Culture.
3. **The coined concept comes from resolving the tension.** It falls out of the strategic logic, not brainstorming.
4. **One idea per slide.** No exceptions in the strategy section.
5. **Bold, declarative copy.** Headlines do the work. Body copy supports, doesn't repeat.
6. **Cultural proof is mandatory.** Tweets, quotes, real human signals. Not just data.
7. **The exec summary gives away the answer.** The reframe is on page 2. The rest proves it.
8. **Each slide earns the next.** The narrative is a buildup: problem → tension → insight → resolution → concept → how it works.

## Narrative Engine Mapping

The pitch pattern maps cleanly to the existing narrative engines:

| Engine | Where It Lives in the Pitch |
|--------|---------------------------|
| Brand DNA vs Cultural Truth | Slide 7 (The Tension) |
| False Choice → Reframe | Slides 8-11 (Shift → Resolution) |
| Subculture → Mobilization | Slides 6+9 (Audience → Core Truth) |
| Audience Tension → Platform Expansion | Slides 7+12 (Tension → Operationalization) |

The pitch structure is engine-agnostic. Any narrative engine can populate the strategy section.
