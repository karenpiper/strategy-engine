# Research / Strategy Tissue Deck Pattern

Extracted from the user Alexander's Xbox Game Pass Strategy Tissue Session (45 slides, May 2025). This is the benchmark for internal strategy research decks, tissue sessions, and Gate 1 reviews.

## What Makes This Deck Work

### 1. The "Misperception → Reality" Pattern
The core slide pattern: state the misperception as a bold headline (left side), then reveal the reality (right side), with data proof below in cards. Each card has a stat + a real human quote (Reddit, interview) + a green-highlighted takeaway.

**Structure per truth:**
- Slide A: The misperception stated boldly (full slide, huge type, with stat + quote proof below)
- Slide B: Split layout. Left: misperception. Arrow →. Right: reality. Below: three proof cards (stat, quote, highlighted finding)

This two-slide reveal pattern creates setup → payoff for every insight. The audience reads the misperception, feels it, then gets the correction with proof.

### 2. Section Labels as Lenses, Not Topics
Sections aren't named by topic ("Competition," "Audience"). They're named by the type of truth being examined:
- **Product Truths** — What's the reality of the product's perception today?
- **Player Truths** — What are the unmet needs and opportunities of the consumer?
- **Cultural Truths** — What's the current state of gaming culture and youth culture?

Each section has a Venn diagram showing how these three lenses converge into "Our Strategy." The framework makes the research feel purposeful, not encyclopedic.

### 3. Headlines Are Takeaways, Always
Every headline states the conclusion, not the topic. The reader gets the point at headline-scan speed.

Good (from the deck):
- "Game Pass is so much more than what it's known for."
- "They don't want more games, they want more gaming."
- "Gaming is a lifestyle. No longer a behavior."
- "Everyone wants to game more. But gaming today makes them choose between gaming and living."

These are opinionated. They take a position. They're the "so what."

### 4. Real Human Voices as Proof
Almost every data slide includes a direct quote from a real person (Reddit username attributed, interview participant quoted). The quotes aren't decoration. They're evidence that the insight is grounded in what actual humans said, not what an analyst inferred.

Pattern: bold stat card (left) + bold quote card (right) + green-highlighted takeaway card (far right)

### 5. "Universal Truth" Slides as Section Closers
Each section ends with a "Universal [Product/Player/Cultural] Truth" slide. Dark background, huge centered type, one sentence. This is the section's "so what" distilled to its sharpest possible form:
- "Game Pass is so much more than what it's known for."
- "They don't want more games, they want more gaming."

These function like the section opportunity slides but are punchier. One line. No body copy. Maximum impact.

### 6. Copy Simplicity
The copy is brutally simple. Short sentences. Everyday words. No jargon. No consulting-speak. The deck reads like someone talking, not someone writing a report.

Body copy uses:
- Very short paragraphs (1-2 sentences)
- Bold subheads in green to call out key phrases
- Direct address ("We don't have a value problem. We have a perception challenge.")
- Proof quotes attributed to real people by name or username

### 7. Visual Proof (Not Just Data Proof)
The deck includes:
- Memes and social posts as cultural evidence (Spongebob meme about Game Pass)
- Reddit comments screenshotted with attribution
- Video interview stills (real gamers, not stock photos)
- Product screenshots showing the actual experience being discussed

This makes the research feel alive and current, not like a report from a research firm.

## Deck Structure

```
1. Cover (full-bleed game art)
2. Agenda (TODAY: 6 sections numbered)
3. Who's Who (team photos with roles)
4-5. About Us (C&T network)
6. Your Ask (the brief restated, one line, on game art)
7. Game Pass' New Chapter (From → To shifts)
8. How Game Pass Wins (key strategic statement)
9. The Goal (target audience + objective)
10-11. Current State (Truth + Problem, split layout)
12. Research Methodology (three columns: quant, qual, AI)
13. Strategic Framework (Venn: Product Truth + Player Truth + Cultural Truth → Strategy)
14-21. Product Truths (3 misperceptions, each with setup + reveal + Universal Product Truth closer)
22-30. Player Truths (context → insights → real quotes → Universal Player Truth closer)
31-35. Cultural Truths (cultural context → data → Universal Cultural Truth closer)
36-40. The Strategy (synthesis of all three truth lenses)
41-45. Territory Explorations + Next Steps
```

## How to Apply This to Other Research Decks

1. **Name your sections by the type of truth, not the topic.** "Category Truths" not "Category Dynamics." "Audience Truths" not "Target Audience."
2. **Use the Misperception → Reality two-slide pattern** for every key finding.
3. **Include real human voices** (Reddit, Twitter, interview quotes) as proof, not just data.
4. **End every section with a one-line Universal Truth** on a dark centered slide.
5. **Keep headlines as sharp takeaways.** If the headline doesn't state a position, rewrite it.
6. **Simplify ruthlessly.** If a sentence needs reading twice, it's too long.
7. **Show the research methodology** early (slide 12 equivalent) so the audience trusts the source.
