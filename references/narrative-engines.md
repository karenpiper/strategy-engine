# Narrative Engines

A library of strategic narrative structures. Each engine creates a different kind of tension and suits a different brief type. The skill selects the best-fit engine based on the brief, or proposes one for approval.

---

## Engine 1: Brand DNA vs Cultural Truth

**When to use:** Brand positioning, repositioning, brand strategy. When the brand has a strong heritage or founding purpose that needs to meet a changed world.

**The tension:** What the brand was built to do vs what the world now demands.

**Section sequence:**

1. **The Challenge** — The brand's current problem, stated bluntly with cultural specificity
2. **The Ambition** — What success looks like (one sentence)
3. **Brand DNA** — The brand's founding purpose, core belief, or original advantage
4. **Cultural Truth** — How the world has shifted since that founding. The gap between what the brand offers and what people now need
5. **The Key Insight** — The human behavior or cultural signal that resolves the tension. Rooted in how people actually feel, not market data
6. **The Concept** — A coined idea or positioning territory that crystallizes the insight (propose 3-5 options)
7. **The Opportunity** — Where the brand can own space. Often visualized as a competitive map or white space analysis
8. **The Strategy** — How the concept comes to life. The strategic direction in one sentence, then the pillars or expressions

**Example tension (from Bumble):** Bumble was created to empower women through a dating app built around their needs. But a decade later, women's romantic experience is more fraught than ever. The insight: dating apps killed romance, and women are searching for that feeling outside of dating entirely. The concept: Romantic Futurism.

## Engine 2: False Choice → Reframe

**When to use:** Business transformation, GTM strategy, pitching a new model or product. When the industry operates on a binary assumption that limits thinking.

**The tension:** An accepted either/or that stifles growth, resolved by proving both are possible.

**Section sequence:**

1. **Foundational Truth** — The brand's or portfolio's core strength (what makes them credible)
2. **The Problem** — The false binary the industry accepts. Framed as: "Every [process] starts from zero, creating false choices that stifle [outcome]"
3. **False Choice → Our Belief** — Name the false either/or, then state how both combine. Repeat for each false choice (usually 2-3)
4. **The Opportunity** — What becomes possible when you reject the false choices. Often a Venn diagram or convergence model
5. **The Implication** — The brand-specific consequence. "Your [asset] is the advantage no one can [verb]"
6. **The Solution** — Named system, product, or approach. Described in one headline + 3-4 pillars
7. **How It Works** — Mechanics. How the system operates, with a visual metaphor (e.g., orchestration layer, compounding curve)
8. **Proof/Use Cases** — Concrete demonstrations. Pick 2 scenarios, walk through each showing the system in action
9. **The Numbers** — Before/after comparison table. Specific metrics that changed

**Example tension (from Suntory):** Every campaign starts from zero, creating false choices: Scale or Relevance, Low Cost or Performance. Our belief: both combine for market share. The solution: The Machine, an AI-native orchestration layer that compounds every campaign into the next.

## Engine 3: Subculture → Mobilization

**When to use:** Marketing strategy, launch campaigns, cultural positioning. When the brand needs to reach a new audience through community rather than mass media.

**The tension:** Brand credibility with an existing core vs irrelevance with a growth audience, resolved through subcultural authenticity.

**Section sequence:**

1. **The Challenge** — The brand's market problem (new product, new audience, limited budget)
2. **The Reframe** — Instead of going mass, focus on winning the intense loyalty of an influential few
3. **Brand DNA** — What the brand has earned (credibility, excellence, loyalty within its core)
4. **Cultural Truth** — How the category is evolving from a functional activity into a cultural phenomenon
5. **The Cultural Shift** — Why a specific subculture or community is the vehicle for reaching the growth audience. Name the emotional needs it serves (list 4-5 with one-line explanations)
6. **The Opportunity** — The untapped convergence point between communities
7. **Our Strategy** — The activation idea: partner, format, and creative approach. Concrete names, places, communities
8. **The Expression** — Visual references, mood, creative direction

**Example tension (from Brooks):** Brooks' trail shoe needs to win outdoors-loving Gen Z, but they spent all resources on R&D with no youth relevance. The reframe: instead of going mass, mobilize subculture. Running crews are a rare vehicle for belonging and identity. The outdoors are the convergence point between streetwear and running communities. Partner with RSVP Gallery in Chicago.

## Engine 4: Audience Tension → Platform Expansion

**When to use:** Experience strategy, product strategy, service design. When the brief is about expanding what a brand means to its audience beyond its current category.

**The tension:** The audience's emotional needs have outgrown the brand's current product frame.

**Section sequence:**

1. **The Challenge** — Current category fatigue or perception problem
2. **The Ambition** — The expanded role (one sentence: "Position X as a [new frame] for [audience]")
3. **From → To** — A bridge diagram. What the brand is today (Product, Brand, Visibility, Basics) vs what it becomes (Experience, Curator, Impact, Lifestyle)
4. **The Insight** — The deeper human need the brand can serve that transcends its current category
5. **The World Building** — A concept map showing how the core idea branches into multiple dimensions (e.g., Romance as Muse, Mindset, Experience, Belief)
6. **The Strategic Territory** — A 2x2 or positioning map showing where competitors sit and where the white space is
7. **The Concept** — The coined positioning idea (propose 3-5 options)
8. **Expressions** — How the expanded platform comes to life across touchpoints

**Example tension (from Bumble expanded):** Bumble is stuck as a dating app in a category people resent. The ambition: position Bumble as a 360% lifestyle service for modern women. From a brand people recognize to a brand people live by. The insight: romance is bigger than dating. It's a muse, a mindset, an experience, a belief. The concept: Romantic Futurism.

## Adding New Engines

When the user identifies a new narrative structure, add it here with:
- **When to use** (brief type)
- **The tension** (one sentence)
- **Section sequence** (numbered, with labels)
- **Example tension** (from real work)

The engine library should grow over time. Each engine is a reusable structure, not a template. The copy, insight, and concept are always original to the brief.
